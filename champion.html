A Watershed Moment for Rocket Athletics

One building.
Every Rocket.

The Champions Complex is a transformational renovation of the University of Toledo's 74,000-square-foot Health Education Building, strategically reimagined to centralize every dimension of student-athlete development under one roof. For the first time in program history, academics, nutrition, mental health, elite training, and team culture will all exist in a single, modern environment at the heart of our main campus.

This is not simply a facilities upgrade. This is a declaration. When you invest in the Champions Complex, you are directly expanding the capabilities of every Rocket athlete, regardless of sport or scholarship status. You are changing what it means to be a Rocket.

Pillar Four — Year-Round Training

The Indoor Training Turf

Ohio winters no longer dictate the ceiling of Rocket development. The 11,000-square-foot Indoor Training Turf gives baseball, softball, and multi-sport athletes the ability to practice, condition, and compete at full intensity twelve months a year. The Human Return on Investment

A Day in the Life
of a Rocket Athlete

The Champions Complex is not a collection of rooms. It is a carefully designed daily environment that removes friction, saves time, and creates the conditions where both athletic and academic excellence become inevitable.

7:00 AM — Morning

Fuel. Strategize. Connect.

The day begins in the Performance Dining Center. Athletes fuel for the demands ahead with sport-specific nutrition plans curated by on-site dietitians. Coaches share early film notes over the team tables. In a single space, preparation has already begun. No van ride to a remote dining hall. No wasted time. Just Rockets, together.

9:00 AM — Mid-Morning

Study Hall. Tutoring. Graduation.

One floor up, the Academic Center is alive with focused energy. Athletes move between collaborative open workstations and private glass-walled tutoring rooms. Academic advisors are embedded in the space, not a separate building across campus. The result: higher grade point averages, greater graduation rates, and student-athletes who leave Toledo genuinely prepared for life beyond sport.

2:00 PM — Afternoon

Recovery. Wellness. Mental Strength.

The Student-Athlete Wellness Suite provides access to sports psychologists, mental performance coaches, and recovery specialists. In a modern collegiate landscape where mental health is as critical as physical conditioning, this embedded access removes the stigma and the commute, ensuring every Rocket has the full support system they need to perform at the highest level and thrive as a human being.

4:00 PM — Practice

Year-Round. Every Day. No Excuses.

The 11,000-square-foot Indoor Training Turf does not care about the weather outside. Baseball and softball athletes work on hitting mechanics, fielding repetitions, and conditioning drills throughout January, February, and March, months that previously rendered outdoor development impossible. This is the competitive difference that changes rosters, changes records, and changes the trajectory of a program.

A Campus Landmark

More Than a Building.
A Campus Statement.

The Champions Complex does not exist in isolation. It is architected to integrate seamlessly into the pedestrian experience of our main campus, featuring outdoor gathering spaces, yellow Toledo-branded seating, vibrant landscaping, and a towering Rocket sculpture that will become an iconic landmark for alumni, prospective students, and the broader Toledo community. The investment extends beyond the walls.

By reclaiming a centralized, underutilized campus building and returning our baseball and softball programs from the geographically isolated Scott Park facility, we are not just building a better athletic complex. We are building a more unified, more vibrant, and more competitive university.
